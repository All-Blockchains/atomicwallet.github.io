What is XRP?

We bet you’ve heard about XRP, one of the major players in the cryptocurrency world. XRP is the the world’s 3rd largest crypto that fuels Ripple Network’s ecosystem.

Originally called Ripplepay, XRP was created in 2004 by Ryan Fugge. The initial idea of was to create an alternative distributed monetary system enabling individuals and enterprises to create their own currency. In turn, this concept gave birth to another principle: unlike Bitcoin, Ripple was designed as a payment system that doesn’t rely on blockchain ledgers and centralized trading platforms.

In 2012, Fugge and his team created OpenCoin Inc that later was renamed Ripple Labs. Ripple Transaction Protocol (RTXP), released by Ripple Labs was designed to enable two parties to send and receive money with exceptional transaction speed. This technology has found large prominence in the banking sphere.

XRP and traditional banks

From 2014, Ripple focuses on the banking market and aims to disrupt the traditional payment system. The Ripple protocol was adopted by a range of financial institutions, including Fidor Bank, Cross River Bank and Kansas-based CBW Bank. In 2018, the Earthport payment service started working in combination with Ripple, as well.

What is XRP?

XRP is an underlying coin of Ripple. All transactions in the Ripple network are powered by XRP. XRP is designed to be a mediator during the exchange processes in both cryptocurrencies and regular (fiat) money. For example, if you want to exchange EUR to USD, Ripple becomes both EUR and USD. On the Ripple network, such a transaction costs $0.00001, and this amount is non-replenishable, i.e. with every single transaction made via the Ripple network, the world loses $0.00001. For that reason, spamming becomes senseless.

XRP is one of the fastest and more scalable blockchains on the market nowadays that can become a competitor to the Visa or Mastercard when comparing to the TPS(transactions per second).

XRP uses a proof-of-stake consensus algorithm. On the most blockchains PoS is handled by stakeholders. They put their funds to the escrow account to be able to vote and receive rewards for the block creation and transactions processing.

Takeaways

Ripple is a disruptive payment system aiming to change the way we interact with money. XRP, Ripple’s native coin, provides fast and secure transactions between peers. XRP becomes more and more popular among millions of users, from individuals to banks. This is one of the most appealing cryptocurrencies on the market.
