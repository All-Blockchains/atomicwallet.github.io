Dash Architecture

Dash architecture consists of two parts. At the lower level are the miners, the second level is given under the master node - the nodes that confirm the transaction. This mechanism is called PrivateSend - “private delivery”. The principle of a two-tier architecture allows completely anonymous transactions. The system does not store data about who receives and makes transfers, and it is impossible to calculate them during network operation.

All transactions in Dash are grouped on the basis of the amount - approximately equal transfers are sent to one category. Next, the master node mixes the dispatch with each other. As a result, the senders and recipients are mixed together after the first stage of mixing, and it does not work out what happened before. The more rounds, the more likely that no one will receive information about who sent the Dash tokens and who received them. Users can set any number of mixes in the range from 2 to 8. Due to the fact that mixing is carried out in advance, the transaction time in Dash does not exceed 2-3 minutes. As soon as the mixing is completed, the owner of the tokens receives them to new addresses and can use them whenever he wants.

A master node is a computer that shuffles transactions. Any member of the network can become the owner of the master node: all you need to do is send a request and pay a deposit of 1000 DASH (approximately $ 1 million at the current rate).

Such a high amount was not chosen by chance: it guarantees that no one will be able to control more than 51% of the masters and learn all the data about the senders and recipients of transactions.

However, such a significant “entry threshold” in the creation of a master node does not stop the participants. This is explained by high profitability: 45% of net revenues go to payments to users whose devices confirm transactions. Payment for each block with records of transfers - 5 DASH, and from them to the owners of the master node “drips” in the amount of 2.25 tokens.
