What is BCH?

Bitcoin Cash (BCH) is one of the top cryptocurrencies nowadays. Plenty of companies accept it as a payment. You can get a ticket to a plane, rent a car, buy food, invest, or you can exchange it to the other cryptocurrencies.

Bitcoin Cash is a fork of Bitcoin. In other words, it is a new cryptocurrency born from the original Bitcoin blockchain. Unlike Bitcoin, Bitcoin Cash has a significant difference called "block size". In the Bitcoin Cash blockchain, the block size is 32MB against 2MB Bitcoin block size. The increased block size allows to handle over 8 millions transactions per day, whereas the Bitcoin blockchain capacity is only 250,000. High improvement, isn't it?

What is a hard fork?

Fork means program code modification. The whole blockchain will be getting on the new rails. The way a fork works is instead of creating a totally new cryptocurrency (and blockchain), a fork just creates a duplicated version with the same history but now with the new rules of its network.

What happens to those who had funds on their addresses before the hard fork? They are lucky ones! Their accounts will be doubled.

What means no backward compatibility?

You have two ledgers: the old one and new one. All records will be copied and transferred from the old to the new one with one main rule: If you want to interact with the new chain, you must install the update.

What significant changes come with Bitcoin Cash?

  • Block size (32 MB)
  • No SegWit support
  • All the data is stored in blockchain only. And no other parties have any access to it.
  • No priority with higher fees
  • No mining pool control

The systems make changes in the block mining difficulty to attract miners and prevent blockchain from creating mining pools that may have control over the system. The Median Time Past which shows the time when the last 11 blocks were created to predict the time when the future block will be created. If the Median Time Past of the current block and the Median Time Past of 6 blocks before is greater than 12 hours then the difficulty reduces by 20% i.e. it becomes 20% easier for miners to find newer blocks.

  • SigHash algorithm

Bitcoin Cash has a Replay Attack protection, sighash algorithm which helps to prevent users from sending transactions on the non-compatibility address. Like BTC instead of BCH for example. The problem is because the forks are working on the similar blockchain but with different protocols.

What was the reason for the hard fork?

The key reason is the scalability issues of the Bitcoin blockchain. The block stores transactions and when is filled, the other block is needed for a new record in the ledger. Hard fork appears because the Bitcoin community (stakeholders and miners) could not come up with a consensus.

How to increase the network capacity and make Bitcoin more scalable?

If you increase the block size, the transaction fees will be decreased. It affects miners that propagate the whole Bitcoin network all over the globe and want to keep their income level stable.

There also were discussions that SegWit (Segregation Witness) can store an account's data and slightly increase the block's capacity. SegWit is being largely criticized from the community for that reason. Would you trust another service to store some of your data if it contradicts the idea of decentralization in crypto?

Block mining problem

Block mining is a complicated process which takes a lot of time and energy, so it's rewarded. Also, the miner, who found a block receives fee payments for each transaction. In 2017, Bitcoin was overloaded with transactions and sometimes the users waited for days to complete one. There also were speculations with fees, in case of making a transaction processing faster.

As a reward is halved in every 210,000 blocks and block mining difficulties constantly increasing, the mining pools have been created, which are always interested in high fees and profits.

There were some attempts to implement changes in the Bitcoin protocol, to increase block size, but they were not successful. Miner community wasn’t interested in making any changes in block rates, cause it may lead to lowering fee costs.

To solve the aforestated problem Bitmain presented Bitcoin Cash as a hard fork of Bitcoin which would make necessary improvements in protocol saving the idea of transparency and decentralization. In November of 2018 Bitcoin Cash has a Hash War initiated by Roger Ver and Craig Wright. Lately, the coin had been hard forked and got a separate Bitcoin SV chain. SV stands for Satoshi Vision, as it is aimed to save the original vision of the Bitcoin Cash network.

What’s Bitcoin SV?

Bitcoin SV has some improvements which make the coin a serious rival to BCH. It aims to:

  • Remove immediate implementation bottlenecks to increasing the block size limit up to 1TB.
  • Lay the technical groundwork for massive future on-chain scaling.
  • Facilitate the transition to smart contracts in the BCHSV network.

If you have BCH in a custodial wallet or on the exchange, where private keys are not under your control – the best way is to move your BCH to a wallet or exchange that supports the fork. If you have Bitcoin Cash (BCH) in a non-custodial wallet where you can manage your private keys – your funds are safe and you'll get an equivalent amount of the new coin after the fork.

Both BCH and BSV are supported on Atomic Wallet, but since there is no replay protection, the transaction will show up in both blockchains. In order to prevent that issue, you will need to split BCH from BSV.
